Why This Works Differently

The entire platform is built on Astro and Cloudflare — the same global infrastructure used by companies like Discord, Shopify, and Canva. Your site is served from over 300 locations worldwide, so visitors always connect to a server near them and pages load in under one second. The database is serverless — it requires no maintenance, no security patches, and no backups from you, ever. AI runs on Cloudflare Workers AI, not a paid API like ChatGPT — so there are no monthly AI fees, no usage caps that surprise you, and no third-party dependency that could change its pricing overnight.

The result: hosting, database, AI, bot protection, and email all cost $0 per month. The only ongoing expense is your domain name at roughly $15 per year. A comparable WordPress setup costs $75–$160 per month — that's $900 to $1,920 per year — before anyone touches the maintenance.

And when the project is done, you own everything: the code, every credential, the hosting account. There's no lock-in, no proprietary CMS, and nothing that stops working if you walk away.

$15/year vs $900–1,920/year

Cloudflare's free tier handles massive traffic without cost increases. Unlike shared hosting — which slows down or charges overage fees when traffic spikes — your site is served from a global edge network that scales automatically. The free tier has no time limit and no trial period. It's genuinely free, permanently.
